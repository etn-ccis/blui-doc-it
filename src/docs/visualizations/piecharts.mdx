# Pie charts

Pie charts are used for comparing relationships between parts of a whole. The area of a circle is split into slices, each corresponding to a dataset category.

### When to use

Pie charts work well for satisfying the following user needs, among others:

- Displaying different parts within a data category, such as energy sources (e.g. 60% Wind, 20% Solar, 20% Natural Gas);

- Demonstrating how a budget is split;

- Showing how partial values add up to a total.

### Best practices

<p style={{fontSize: '20px',fontWeight: 'bold',}}>How to use the Donut layout</p>

Donut charts are very similar to Pie charts but their central section is removed. That effect enhances the user's perception of proportion, helping the audience better judge the size of each slice. For tips on using the extra space available, see Gauges below.

<ImageGrid
    images={[DonutLayoutDo, DonutLayoutDont]}
    captionsUnderImages={[
        'DO:Removing the centre section improves readability',
        'DONT:Avoid multi-level donut charts for a general audience. Keep it to specialised users only'
    ]}
/>

<p style={{fontSize: '20px',fontWeight: 'bold',}}>How to use the Gauge layout</p>

Gauges are a variation of Donut charts, in which a qualitative value is displayed in the empty center of the chart. Adding a gauge to a scorecard is a known visual pattern, commonly used in dashboards to help users quickly assess quality or deadlines.

<ImageGrid
    images={[GaugeOverallHealth, GaugeWarrantyDates]}

<p style={{fontSize: '20px',fontWeight: 'bold',}}>Keep categories at a minimum</p>

Research has shown that users find it hard to derive meaning from pie charts containing more than four categories. If you have more than four categories within your dataset, consider using [Bar charts](/visualizations/barcharts).

<ImageGrid
    images={[LimitCategories, CrowdedPiechart]}
    captionsUnderImages={[
        'DO:Limit categories for visual cleanliness and easy reading',
        'DONT:Crowded pie charts are difficult to read, even when a tooltip is present'
    ]}
/>

<p style={{fontSize: '20px',fontWeight: 'bold',}}>Display your data in descending order</p>

Ranking the categories in descending order, starting at the 12 o'clock point can help your users to better visualise the relationship between the data.

<ImageGrid
    images={[DescendingOrder, RandomOrder]}

        'DONT:Avoid using random angles for slices'
    ]}
/>
