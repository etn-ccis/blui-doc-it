# BLUI FLEX Awards

The Brightlayer UI FLEX Awards program recognizes individuals and teams across Eaton who have demonstrated outstanding contributions in adopting, promoting, and advancing the Brightlayer UI design system. FLEX stands for the four award categories: F (Frontend Execution), L (Learner), E (Evangelist / Community), and X (eXceptional Contributor).

<Divider />

## How the FLEX Awards Work

The FLEX Awards follow a structured process to ensure fairness and transparency:

1. **Kickoff Meeting** — A meeting is held to introduce the FLEX Awards to all teams, explain the categories, evaluation criteria, and nomination process.
2. **Nomination Form** — A form is circulated via email for team leads and peers to nominate candidates or self-nominate. Nominees provide supporting evidence such as slides, videos, and written descriptions.
3. **Evaluation** — The Brightlayer UI team evaluates all submissions against the defined criteria for each category. Winners are notified prior to the public announcement.
4. **Award Ceremony** — A dedicated meeting is held to announce and celebrate the winners.
5. **Awards** — Winners receive recognition through E-Star nominations, T-shirts, gift cards, hoodies, and water bottles.

## Award Categories

### 1. Contributor Award

Recognizes individuals who have actively contributed to evolving the Brightlayer UI design system between evaluation cycles.

**Nomination Questions:**
- What has been done to help evolve the Brightlayer UI design system?
- Upload 1 slide to further elaborate.
- Any other things the Brightlayer UI team should know when evaluating your submission?

**Evaluation Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Participation | 40% | How frequent was the contribution, and how much effort was spent. |
| Impact | 40% | How much business and community influence the contribution has made or will make. |
| Platforming & Scale | 20% | Whether this is a one-off contribution or a pattern that can be scaled and extended to fit other projects' needs. |

<Divider />

### 2. Community Award

Recognizes individuals who have gone above and beyond to help other teams adopt and succeed with Brightlayer UI.

**Nomination Questions:**
- Is this nomination for a Designer or Developer?
- How has the nominee helped other teams?
- Upload 1 slide to further elaborate.
- Any other things the Brightlayer UI team should know when evaluating your submission?

**Evaluation Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Participation | 40% | How frequent was the contribution, and how much effort was spent. |
| Community Impact | 60% | How much community influence the contribution has made or will make. |

<Divider />

### 3. Learner Award

Recognizes individuals who have acquired new UI skills (design or development) and applied them effectively in their work.

**Nomination Questions:**
- What UI skills (design or development) has the nominee acquired?
- How did the nominee apply their new skills to help with their everyday work?
- Has the nominee done anything to share the new knowledge with people around them? How?
- Upload 1 slide to further elaborate.
- Any other things the Brightlayer UI team should know when evaluating your submission?

**Evaluation Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Application | 60% | How has the learning been used to improve the nominee's work? |
| Sharing | 25% | How has the nominee inspired others to learn? How did they share their new knowledge or skill with others? |
| Outside the Comfort Zone | 15% | How different is this learning topic from the nominee's everyday work? |

<Divider />

### 4. Execution Award

Recognizes products and teams that have best followed the Brightlayer UI design and development standards in their applications.

**Nomination Questions:**
- Which business division is this product from?
- Who is the contact for this submission?
- List of team members (up to 6) involved in designing and developing the user interface.
- Is the product a new launch or a major innovation?
- What was improved during the major innovation?
- Provide a video link to demonstrate the user interface of the product.
- Provide instructions on how to access the UI source code.
- Upload up to 4 slides to further elaborate.
- Any other things the Brightlayer UI team should know when evaluating your submission?

**Evaluation Criteria:**

The Execution Award is scored across two major dimensions:

#### Design Evaluation (40%)

The design evaluation assesses the visual adherence to Brightlayer UI standards (UX is not evaluated, only UI):

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Components (Choice & Alignment) | 30% | Proper selection and alignment of Brightlayer UI components. |
| Animations | 5% | Quality and appropriateness of UI animations. |
| Colors / Accessibility | 20% | Correct use of the Brightlayer UI color palette and accessibility compliance. |
| Icons | 20% | Proper use of Brightlayer UI iconography. |
| Information Density | 10% | Effective use of space and information layout. |
| New Inventions / Breaking Rules | 15% | Creative deviations from standard patterns where justified. |

#### Development Evaluation (60%)

The development evaluation checks adherence to Brightlayer UI coding best practices:

| Criterion | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Unnecessary Override | Whether the product avoids unnecessary overrides of Brightlayer UI defaults. |
| Encapsulation | Proper encapsulation and code organization. |
| Documentation | Quality of project documentation (README, comments, setup instructions). |
| BLUI Packages | Use of official Brightlayer UI packages (themes, colors, components). |
| Up-to-date | Whether dependencies and Brightlayer UI packages are on current versions. |

## Participation Recognition

All nominees and those who nominate others are recognized with an E-Star for their participation, regardless of whether they win in their category.

<Divider />

## Timeline

The FLEX Awards are conducted annually. The typical timeline includes:

1. **Kickoff & Awareness** — Teams are informed about the awards program and nomination period.
2. **Nomination Window** — A defined period during which nominations are accepted via the online form.
3. **Evaluation Period** — The Brightlayer UI team reviews all submissions and scores them based on category-specific criteria.
4. **Winner Notification** — Winners are informed ahead of the public announcement.
5. **Award Ceremony** — Public announcement and celebration of winners.

## How to Participate

- **Nominate yourself or a peer** — Fill out the nomination form when it is circulated during the nomination window.
- **Provide supporting materials** — Include slides, videos, or code links to strengthen your nomination.
- **Attend the kickoff meeting** — Learn about the current year's categories and expectations.
